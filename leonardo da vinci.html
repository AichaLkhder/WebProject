Mona Lisa

By Leonardo da Vinci

Mona Lisa

A portrait of Lisa Gherardini from 1503–1506, known for her mysterious smile and delicate use of light and shadow, making it one of the most famous works in art history.

The Last Supper

By Leonardo da Vinci

The Last Supper

A large fresco from 1495–1498 depicting the moment when Jesus reveals that one of his disciples will betray him, capturing the emotional reactions of the figures.

Lady with an Ermine

By Leonardo da Vinci

Lady with an Ermine

A portrait from around 1489–1490 of Cecilia Gallerani, showcasing Leonardo's mastery of texture and light, with the figure holding an ermine, which symbolized purity.

Salvator Mundi

By Leonardo da Vinci

Salvator Mundi

A painting from around 1500 of Christ holding a crystal orb, often interpreted as symbolizing his dominion over the world, recently gaining attention for its controversial sale.

Head of a Woman

By Leonardo da Vinci

Head of a Woman

An unfinished portrait from around 1500–1508 of a serene young woman with disheveled hair, showcasing Leonardo's mastery of sfumato and his focus on delicate human expression.
